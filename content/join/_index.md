# Join DS@GT ARC

If you want to get involved with DS@GT Applied Research Competitions, start here.

The [Spring 2026 CLEF Competition](/join/spring-2026/) cycle is currently underway. For most new students, the next main entry point will be Fall 2026 recruitment rather than joining mid-cycle.

## Current status

- **Spring 2026:** active and underway.
- **Best path for most new members:** plan for Fall 2026 recruitment.
- **If you want updates now:** join the [DS@GT Slack channel](https://datasciencegt.slack.com/?redir=%2Farchives%2FC05MW3LPZFZ%3Fname%3DC05MW3LPZFZ) or use [Contact](/contact-us/).

## Best path for most students

1. Review the [FAQ](/faq/) to understand expectations, roles, and time commitment.
2. Join the [DS@GT Slack channel](https://datasciencegt.slack.com/?redir=%2Farchives%2FC05MW3LPZFZ%3Fname%3DC05MW3LPZFZ) if you want updates on future recruitment.
3. Visit [Contact](/contact-us/) if you still need help reaching the right person.
4. Explore [Impact](/impact/) and [Publications](/publications/) to see what ARC teams produce.
5. Browse the archived [Fall 2025 Interest Group](/join/fall-2025/) page if you want to understand how the onboarding cycle typically works.

## Which path should you choose?

- **Most new members:** wait for the next Fall recruitment cycle and follow updates through Slack or Contact.
- **Current Spring 2026 participants:** use the [Spring 2026 CLEF Competition](/join/spring-2026/) page as a reference for the active cycle.
- **Still unsure:** read the [FAQ](/faq/) and then use [Contact](/contact-us/).

## Eligibility and expectations at a glance

ARC is open to Georgia Tech students across degree programs, including online students. Members are expected to join the broader DS@GT organization, pay dues, and contribute consistently. For most students, the fall semester is the right place to build the skills and context needed for spring competition work.
