# Join DS@GT ARC

If you want to get involved with DS@GT Applied Research Competitions, start here.

Most new members begin with the [Fall 2025 Interest Group](/join/fall-2025/), which is designed to help students build the background needed for ARC's spring competition teams. Returning members and qualified students can then continue into the [Spring 2026 CLEF Competition](/join/spring-2026/).

## Best path for most students

1. Read the [Fall 2025 Interest Group](/join/fall-2025/) page.
2. Review the [FAQ](/faq/) to understand expectations, roles, and time commitment.
3. Join the [DS@GT Slack channel](https://datasciencegt.slack.com/?redir=%2Farchives%2FC05MW3LPZFZ%3Fname%3DC05MW3LPZFZ) if you want to ask questions.
4. Visit [Contact](/contact-us/) if you still need help reaching the right person.
5. Explore [Impact](/impact/) and [Publications](/publications/) to see what ARC teams produce.

## Which path should you choose?

- **Most new members:** start with the [Fall 2025 Interest Group](/join/fall-2025/).
- **Returning members or qualified students:** review the [Spring 2026 CLEF Competition](/join/spring-2026/) page.
- **Still unsure:** read the [FAQ](/faq/) and then use [Contact](/contact-us/).

## Current opportunities

- [Fall 2025 Interest Group](/join/fall-2025/) — the main onboarding path for new members.
- [Spring 2026 CLEF Competition](/join/spring-2026/) — for returning members and qualified students preparing to compete and publish.

## Eligibility and expectations at a glance

ARC is open to Georgia Tech students across degree programs, including online students. Members are expected to join the broader DS@GT organization, pay dues, and contribute consistently. For most students, the fall semester is the right place to build the skills and context needed for spring competition work.
